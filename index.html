Invisibles

Text can carry characters you can't see — zero-width spaces, look-alike spaces, direction-flipping controls, AI watermark characters. Paste it below to reveal them, then strip them with one click.

Everything here runs in your browser with JavaScript. This page makes no network requests — open your dev tools and watch. Your text is never sent anywhere.

Why it matters

A single invisible character can quietly break things. It kept a PlayStation trophy unobtainable for ten years. It corrupts JSON and CSV imports, breaks password fields and code search, and slips fake data into analytics. Direction-control characters can even reorder source code so it does something different from what you read — the "Trojan Source" attack. And text pasted out of an AI often carries tell-tale characters like the narrow no-break space (U+202F) that most editors never show you.

What it catches

Zero-width

Zero-width spaces, joiners, word joiners, and the BOM — fully invisible, and the ones that break search and passwords.

Look-alike spaces

NBSP, narrow no-break space, ideographic and thin spaces — they look like a normal space but aren't.

Direction controls

Right-to-left overrides and isolates that can visually reorder or hide text (Trojan Source).

Hidden / tag chars

Invisible "tag" characters and variation selectors used to smuggle hidden text, prompts, or watermarks.

Nothing is uploaded

Most "remove invisible characters" and "AI text cleaner" tools are web services — you paste sensitive text and it goes to their server. This one doesn't have a server. The whole thing is a single page and one small script you can read in a couple of minutes. It works offline, and it stays free.
